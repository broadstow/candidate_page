Croydon's Way Ahead

Val Shawcross, Former Deputy London Mayor and Croydon Council Leader, to Seek Labour Nomination for Croydon’s New Mayoralty


As a Croydon resident who has lived in the London Borough of Croydon since 1985, I’m announcing my intention to seek selection as the Labour candidate at next May’s Croydon mayoral election.

Val Shawcross for Croydon

Thank you to all three Croydon CLPs for your nominations. I am now the only candidate with nominations covering the entire Borough.

Former Deputy London Mayor and Croydon Council Leader to Seek Labour Nomination for Croydon’s New Mayoralty

As Croydon Council Leader in the late 1990s I introduced Croydon’s tram system before becoming Chair of the Assembly Transport Committee and then Deputy Mayor of London for Transport to Sadiq Khan, working up key schemes such as the Ultra-Low Emission Zone to clean up London's air. I also spent eight years as Chair of the London Fire Brigade.

My Pledge

“A Labour Council should listen to local people and create opportunities for community involvement in running local neighbourhoods and facilities like parks. We need, as a community, to start tackling the problems of the current era. People are worried about energy costs, issues like crime, fly tipping and our local and global environment."

“Incompetent decision making by Conservative National Government is impacting badly on our businesses, residents and local public services.

“I believe that I’ve had enough successful political and management experience in London to make a difference to Croydon and the lives of everyone who lives here and I want a chance to bring what I’ve learned over my 25 years in public life back to Croydon. I’m committed to Croydon. It's a great place to live and we should be moving it forwards.”
